Questions about our website? Start here!

Why is vaccination important for puppies?
Vaccination protects against diseases such as Parvo, Distemper, and Rabies.

How often should I vaccinate?
Puppies: every 3–4 weeks until 16 weeks old. Adults: boosters every 1–3 years.

Can vaccination have side effects?
Mild fever, swelling, or tiredness may occur but usually fade in 24 hours.

How often should I bathe my dog?
Once every 4–6 weeks is best.

What’s the role of grooming?
Prevents tangles, reduces shedding, and detects skin issues.

What cleaning products are safe?
Use pet-safe shampoos. Avoid bleach/strong chemicals.

When to start training?
At 7–8 weeks with basics.

How to stop chewing and biting?
Redirect to chew toys, reward calm behavior.

How to train for loneliness?
Gradual absences, toy puzzles, calm departures.

What should I feed my puppy?
High-quality puppy food is recommended, rich in protein and nutrients.

How often should puppies eat?
8–12 weeks: 4 meals/day. 3–6 months: 3 meals/day. 6+ months: 2 meals/day.

Are human foods safe for dogs?
Most human foods can be harmful. Stick to dog-safe treats and foods.

How to socialize a puppy?
Introduce to new people, pets, and environments gradually.

What if my puppy is aggressive?
Identify triggers and use positive reinforcement training.

How do I manage barking?
Reward quiet behavior and redirect attention when barking starts.

What are common puppy illnesses?
Parvo, Distemper, and worms are common; vaccination helps prevent many.

When should I call a vet?
High fever, vomiting, diarrhea, lethargy, or sudden changes in behavior.

Basic first aid tips?
Clean wounds with mild antiseptic, prevent licking, and seek vet care if severe.

Can I travel with my puppy?
Yes, with proper crate, breaks, and hydration.

How to keep my puppy safe outdoors?
Leash walks, vaccinations, and avoidance of dangerous areas.

Travel essentials?
Water, portable bowl, first aid kit, favorite toys, and blanket.

When do puppies start teething?
Usually 3–6 months of age.

How to manage chewing?
Provide chew toys, redirect inappropriate chewing, and reward positive behavior.

Can teething cause problems?
Mild gum irritation is normal, but persistent bleeding requires vet attention.

What should I know before adopting a puppy?
Consider budget, time commitment, home environment, and breed needs.

How do I prepare my home?
Create a safe space, puppy-proof areas, provide toys, bed, and leash.

How do I bond with my new puppy?
Regular play, positive reinforcement, consistent routines, and gentle training.
