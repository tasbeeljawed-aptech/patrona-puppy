Types Of Dogs

Dogs are divided into different types such as working dogs, hunting dogs, herding dogs, and companion dogs. Working dogs are used for guarding and rescue, while hunting dogs help in tracking. Herding dogs manage livestock, and companion dogs are kept as pets for love and friendship.

Explore By Types

Working Dogs

Dogs bred for jobs like pulling sleds, guarding property.

Behaviour Characteristics
Protective Strong, muscular build
Hardworking Excellent stamina & endurance
Loyal Independent yet trainable

Guard Dogs

Specialized in protecting families, homes, or livestock.

Behaviour Characteristics
Alert Strong jaw & deep bark
Territorial Highly protective instincts
Courageous Large & powerful body

Companion Dogs

Primarily bred for companionship and family life.

Behaviour Characteristics
Affectionate Small to medium size
Friendly Adapts well to homes
Playful Low to moderate exercise needs

Herding Dogs

Dogs with instincts to herd and control livestock.

Behaviour Characteristics
Energetic Athletic & agile
Intelligent Quick learners, high obedience
Focused Excellent work ethic

Sporting Dogs

Dogs bred for hunting, retrieving, and assisting hunters.

Behaviour Characteristics
Friendly Medium to large build
Active High stamina, loves water
Trainable Sharp senses & instincts

Non-Sporting Dogs

A diverse group with unique sizes, coats, and personalities.

Behaviour Characteristics
Adaptable Various body shapes
Independent Different coat types
Companionable Unique personalities

Toy Dogs

Small-sized dogs bred mainly for companionship.

Behaviour Characteristics
Lively Tiny, lightweight
Playful Big personalities in small bodies
Affectionate Great for apartment living

Hound Dogs

Dogs bred for hunting using sight or scent.

Behaviour Characteristics
Independent Strong hunting instincts
Persistent Excellent sense of smell
Athletic Lean, muscular bodies
